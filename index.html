The frame

Most consulting firms would tell you to put an LLM on top of your support inbox and call it a day. That is the wrong starting point.

The right starting point is a sort. Of the problems on the Arovia team's plate:

60%

Plain software and database work.

Channel reconciliation between Shopify, Kickstarter, and distributor POs. Postgres, not prompts.

30%

Rule-based logic.

Backer-comment triage. Patent-workflow routing. Decisions a flowchart can hold.

10%

Genuine agent problems.

Drafting office-action responses. Summarizing long Kickstarter update threads. Where LLMs earn their cost.

Teams your size lose months when they put an LLM on the 60% instead of a Postgres table. We come in and tell you which layer each piece belongs on, starting with the two or three workflows costing you the most weekly hours.

Case study

Eduba built a multi-agent compliance system for a mining-sector client that compressed 160+ hours of manual document work into under 5 minutes. The paper behind the method

Interpretable Context Methodology. Folder structure as agent architecture.

Jake published the methodology in ACM TiiS. It is the right frame for a team that cannot afford a full data platform but needs their channel, support, and IP workflows to be navigable by both humans and agents. Start with git-versioned folders. Layer agent scaffolding on top.
